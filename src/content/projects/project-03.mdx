## Project Overview

HomeGrown is a team-built hackathon project that helps people start sustainable home gardens by recommending the right crops for their region, tracking plant growth milestones, and planning future marketplace and disease-detection features.

## My Role

Our team started by researching climate change impacts on urban gardening and surveying real gardeners to identify pain points. From there, we started to build prototypes to address the core challenges: crop selection, monitoring, and community connection.

My contributions focused on building functional prototypes using JavaScript and React to demonstrate crop recommendations, growth tracking, and usability for real-world environmental data. 

## How It Works

Users create a profile with their gardening experience and location. The app then recommends crops based on local temperature, humidity, pH, and rainfall data.

It sets growth milestones with reminders and instructions, and lays the groundwork for future features like plant disease diagnosis via AI and a marketplace for buying, selling, or bartering home-grown crops.

## Technical Challenges

The main challenge was turning extensive research into a working prototype under hackathon time constraints. We conducted user surveys to create personas, analyzed climate data challenges, and prototyped solutions for crop recommendation and lifecycle tracking.

Data scarcity made it hard to train models fully, so we focused on a JavaScript prototype that proved the concept while planning for future data collection and community features.

## Impact

The project promotes urban gardening to combat climate change effects on food crops and encourage more home planting to reduce carbon emissions. By making gardening accessible, it helps people grow their own food and build sustainable habits.
