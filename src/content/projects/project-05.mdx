## Project Overview

My Warrior’s Shield is a team-built hackathon project designed to help young supporters of breast cancer warriors feel more empowered and less overwhelmed. The app lets users create custom task lists, track mood and symptoms, and access curated guidance and support resources.

## My Role

Our team met and communicated daily to shape the product idea, plan the experience, and divide development work. After the brainstorming phase, I created the page diagram in Figma, suggested using Wix because of its customization capabilities, and worked with Sunaina to start building the site.

I created the website structure, including the member sites, core pages, required links, and the connection between user input forms and profile data. I also helped connect the dashboard logic so the app could call and display stored information through Wix data and APIs.

## How It Works

Users can log in with Google, Facebook, or email and set up a profile for their warrior. The app then takes them to a personalized dashboard where they can add tasks, check off completed actions, and record daily mood check-ins.

The dashboard also includes lightboxes with support information, breast cancer resources, self-care guidance, and suggestions for what to say and what not to say. These features were designed for younger users, especially ages 13 to 18, who want to support a loved one but may not know how.

## Technical Challenges

One of the biggest challenges was that the original development plan had to be scrapped after a backend developer dropped out. Instead of delaying the project, the team pivoted to Wix so we could still deliver a working prototype on time.

That shift required us to adapt our scope, rethink privacy concerns, and balance functionality with platform limitations. It also pushed me to learn more about Wix, its built-in APIs, and how no-code tools can still support meaningful full-stack style product work.

## Tech Stack
Wix, Figma, JavaScript, Wix Data Collections, Wix APIs, Member Pages

## Impact
The project was built to help young caregivers feel supported, informed, and capable of helping their loved one through treatment. Rather than leaving users feeling helpless, the app gives them structured ways to help, check in, and stay connected.
